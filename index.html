Our Solution

Blue Drop creates ultra-low-cost water systems that pull clean water from air and fog —without electricity, without complex parts, and built to last over 5 years.

How It Works:

We combine bamboo frameworks, fog nets, and old tech to capture and clean water from the environment. It's sustainable, affordable, and easy to assemble anywhere.

Why It Matters:

Every Blue Drop kit can provide lifesaving water access to an entire household—no grid, no pipes, no wait.

Fog Capture

Our specialized fog nets capture water droplets from humid air, providing a reliable water source even in coastal desert regions.

Easy assembly

Our fog nets use the power of physics and smart disgn to harvest water in the air without electricity or complex machinery.

Sustainable Materials

Built primarily from bamboo and other locally-sourced materials, our systems are environmentally friendly and can be repaired using local resources.

About BlueDrop

BlueDrop is revolutionizing water harvesting technology to make clean water accessible to communities worldwide. Our innovative approach reduces costs and environmental impact while maximizing efficiency.

Founded with a mission to solve the global water crisis, we combine cutting-edge technology with sustainable practices to create solutions that work for people and the planet.

Our Vision

"A world where every person has reliable access to clean, safe drinking water, regardless of their location or economic status. We envision communities empowered by sustainable water solutions that respect our planet while meeting humanity's most basic need."

Meet Our Founder

The vision and passion behind BlueDrop's mission

Alya Saad

"I founded BlueDrop with a single thought: water is a human right; no one should struggle for it."

A 15-year-old innovator on a mission to solve water scarcity using low-cost, sustainable tech. Alya combines science, empathy, and DIY spirit to bring clean water to communities that need it most. She believes real change starts simple—and starts now.

After witnessing firsthand the devastating effects of water scarcity during her visits to her home country, Lebanon, she decided to solve this wieghing problem.
